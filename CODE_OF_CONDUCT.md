# Code of Conduct

Ratatosk is an open-source project. We want it to be a place where people can collaborate productively, disagree technically, and still treat each other with respect.

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, chat, documentation, and any other community channels maintained for the project.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, direct, and constructive.
- Focus criticism on ideas, code, and decisions, not on people.
- Keep feedback specific, actionable, and grounded in the project.
- Assume good faith, especially when clarifying mistakes or misunderstandings.
- Be mindful that contributors have different levels of experience, backgrounds, and communication styles.
- Help keep discussions relevant, technically useful, and accessible to others who may read them later.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or threats.
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory language.
- Discriminatory or hateful conduct based on personal characteristics.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances.
- Trolling, deliberate provocation, or repeated bad-faith engagement.
- Publishing private or identifying information without explicit permission.
- Spamming, derailing discussions, or otherwise making collaboration harder for others.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within project spaces and in public interactions that are directly connected to the project or reasonably affect the safety and participation of its community.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this code of conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other contributions that violate these standards.

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, report it privately to the project maintainers through GitHub or any published maintainer contact channel. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as discreetly as reasonably possible.

## Consequences

Maintainers may take any action they believe is appropriate for the situation, including:

- A private warning.
- A request to stop the behavior.
- Removal of content.
- Temporary suspension from project spaces.
- Permanent ban from participation in the project.

## Common Sense

This document cannot cover every situation. Use good judgment, prioritize respect, and keep the project a place where people can do serious technical work without unnecessary hostility.
